# Hypothesis — Concept Check (Track A)

Committed: 20 June 2026, ~03:00 IST (before any application code)

## The claim
A person who can recite a concept but cannot derive it will, after my tool's
probing and guidance, be able to derive it in their own words — more often than not.
(A "real shift" means they answer the apply-it question in their own words after
guidance, not just repeat the hint back.)

## Numbers (tested on 2 people, at least 1 not coached)

- Proves me right: 2 of 2 people who could NOT derive the concept at first
  produce a genuine, in-their-own-words derivation after the tool's guidance.

- Only a mirror (not an instrument): the tool surfaces nothing they didn't
  already show, OR the "after" answer is just the hint repeated back with no
  real understanding. If neither person produces an own-words derivation, the
  tool only reflected what was already there.

- Kill-number: 0 of 2 show a genuine shift → I stop defending the idea and
  write "my hypothesis was wrong."

## What does NOT count as a win
- "It was helpful / nice tool" — opinion, ignored.
- Repeating my hint back verbatim — that's parroting the hint, not understanding.
- A person who could already derive it before guidance — the tool added nothing.
